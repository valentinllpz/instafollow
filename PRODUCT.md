# Product

## Register

brand-product

## Users

Instagram users (18–35) who are socially aware and want a quick, honest audit of who isn't following them back. They arrive with mild anxiety or curiosity, want a fast answer, and care that their data doesn't leave their device. They are not technical — they should never have to think about file formats.

## Product Purpose

ghosted. is a privacy-first Instagram follower checker. You upload two export files from Instagram, and it tells you who isn't following you back — entirely client-side, nothing transmitted. Success looks like: user uploads files, sees the list in under 10 seconds, feels the privacy guarantee without having to read about it.

## Brand Personality

Edgy, minimal, private.

The brand has an attitude — it names the situation ("ghosted") without sugarcoating it. It's blunt, confident, and private. The tool's restraint IS the message: no accounts, no servers, no tracking.

## References

Modern dark product/brand design in the space of fitness-tech, IoT branding, and wellbeing apps. Sophisticated dark palettes with deliberate accent use. Clean, confident typography — not aggressive, not corporate. The shots that inspired this: SOMA Adaptive Fitness, Zonex IoT Branding, a mobile wellbeing Routine hero.

## Anti-references

- **Hacker tool / terminal aesthetic**: No terminal-green-on-black, no aggressive monospace everywhere, no CTF / grep output vibe. The data is social, not code.
- **SaaS dashboard**: No pricing tiers, feature grids, hero metrics, or startup scaffolding.
- **Instagram-branded**: No pink gradients, colorful icon rings, or anything that looks like it belongs inside the Instagram app.

## Design Principles

1. **Privacy is felt, not announced** — the "runs locally" guarantee should be embedded in the experience itself (no loading spinners that suggest a server call, immediate feedback, no network feel), not just stated in footer copy.
2. **Edgy through restraint, not loudness** — attitude comes from confident typographic choices and a name, not from harsh colors or aggressive layout.
3. **Blunt and fast** — the tool gets out of the way. No onboarding rituals, no congratulatory flows, no false warmth.
4. **Minimal surface, maximum signal** — every element earns its place. If removing it wouldn't confuse the user, remove it.
5. **Data treated with gravity** — social follower data is personal. The design should feel like it takes that seriously: spare, controlled, never gamified.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements. Reduced-motion alternative for all animations (crossfade or instant). Sufficient contrast on all text, including muted/placeholder states.
